Cyclistic Bike-Share Case Study

Tools: R, Tableau

Role: Data Cleaning, Analysis, Dashboard Design

Introduction

This case study analyzes 12 months of ride data from Cyclistic, a fictional bike-share program in Chicago, to uncover differences in usage between casual riders and annual members. The goal is to provide data-driven recommendations to convert more casual users into loyal members.

Problems

Casual riders tend to ride less frequently and mostly on weekends, while members ride more consistently during weekdays. The company lacks a clear understanding of what drives these behavioral differences and how they relate to seasonality and ride duration.

Solutions

I used R to clean and combine 12 separate CSVs into a single dataset, removing invalid durations, parsing datetime fields, and creating new columns such as ride length, day of week, and month. Tableau Public was then used to visualize weekly patterns, ride length differences, and peak station usage.

Conclusion

Casual riders take longer rides, prefer weekends, and spike in summer. Members ride shorter distances, more frequently, and during weekdays—indicating routine use. These patterns highlight two distinct customer personas.

Next Steps

Recommend targeting casual users with summer promotions and weekend loyalty discounts. Promote membership benefits focused on commute-time convenience. Focus location-based campaigns on top casual rider start stations.

Retail Sales Analysis (Excel Dashboard)

Tools: Microsoft Excel

Role: Data Cleaning, Pivot Table Analysis, Dashboard Design

Introduction

This project involved cleaning and analyzing fictional retail sales data for various snack products across U.S. cities. The objective was to uncover sales patterns by product type, region, and sales representative.

Problems

Sales managers lacked visibility into which product categories, cities, and team members were driving revenue. Without this insight, performance tracking and promotional targeting were limited.

Solutions

I cleaned the data in Excel, used PivotTables to summarize performance by month, product, and region, and created an interactive dashboard showing sales by category, salesperson, and location. Charts included bar graphs, pie charts, and trend lines.

Conclusion

The dashboard showed that cookies and bars made up over 80% of sales, with Boston and New York leading in revenue. Sales spiked early in the year, indicating potential seasonality. These insights can inform inventory, staffing, and regional promotions.

Next Steps

Recommend focusing future sales efforts on high-performing reps and products in top regions. The dashboard will be updated monthly to reflect trends and guide decisions.
